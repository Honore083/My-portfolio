LISTENING TO PODCASTS

Why Do I Listen To Podcasts

I happen to have found a few people from whom I've learned much about life. These are the late Dr. Paul Farmer, Robert Greene, Dr. Andrew Huberman, and Dr. Cal Newport. In fact, I call them my surrogate fathers rather than role models, as the life advice they freely share with me feels akin to father-to-son wisdom. Their perspectives range from Robert Greene's insights on living a meaningful and fulfilling life, to Andrew Huberman's guidance on optimizing focus and productivity, to Cal Newport's lessons on proper time management and avoiding distractions.

I listen to a new episode of their podcasts each day or at least once every three days during hectic periods, and I aim to reach 100 episodes by the end of this year. It gives me great pleasure to uncover protocols like the Non-Sleep Deep Rest (NSDR) and working in ultradian cycles, as discussed on the Huberman Lab Podcast. These protocols have been helpful in restoring my mental vigor as I work on cognitively-demanding tasks, improving my memory, and boosting other areas of my life. Dr. Huberman's lessons truly feel like actual neuroscience courses.
