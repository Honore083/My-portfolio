What I Do In Engineering

I see Engineering as a window to solving multiple global health problems, particularly through the development of different biomaterials including the biosensors that detect changes in glucose levels of individuals with Diabetes. In fact, this has been a pivotal step towards mitigating the risks associated with major Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs) specifically Diabetes. Therefore, I view engineering as a perfect lens to advance medicine.

Expanding on my NUDOR research topic, I recently developed a machine learning model that connects Parkinson's patients with peer support groups while tracking medication adherence and recommending personalized science-based exercises to reduce tremors. The platform now serves around 200 Rwandan patients, providing data-driven exercise protocols and community-based recovery support.

I have also developed a model that helps visually impaired individuals to navigate their environments independently. The model is based on developing smart glasses with essential features like navigation guidance, reading texts, and making emergency calls. I look forward to working on the hardware part of this project while at college.
