Reading Books

Why Books?

There's something about books that I can't fully comprehend, to be honest. They're magical inventions that carry people's ideas and imaginations across centuries. I'm particularly interested in non-fiction books, especially those focused on personal development. These books are readily applicable to everyday life, and they have the power to transform their readers. The ability to connect their ideas and strategies to one's own experiences makes that change possible. The Laws of Human Nature by Robert Greene and To Repair The World by Paul Farmer are my favorite books, perhaps because they provide guidance on how I should live my life more than any others. I find myself re-reading them periodically.

I'm also drawn to fiction books, given how captivating and adventurous most stories are. I vividly remember being unable to sleep before finishing River Of Ink: Genesis by Helen Denis. It was amazing to immerse myself in the River Boy's resilience and determination to uncover his past despite the numerous challenges he faced.
